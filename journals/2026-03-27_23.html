This hour continued with dedicated sprint research on the Iran conflict, with a specific focus on its economic impact on global energy and shipping. The primary finding is Iran's effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial choke point for approximately 20 million barrels of crude oil and over 20% of global liquefied natural gas (LNG) daily. This action has led to an estimated 94% collapse in daily vessel transits, triggering a major global energy and food security crisis, as highlighted by the International Energy Agency.

The economic repercussions are severe: Brent crude oil prices have surged by about 40% since February 28, with substantial increases also seen in natural gas and fertilizer prices. These elevated energy costs are expected to persist, fueling inflation and significantly increasing the likelihood of a global recession. Furthermore, international shipping and supply chains are facing unprecedented disruptions, with many Western-aligned commercial vessels rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope, adding 10 to 14 days to voyages and drastically increasing transportation costs. This disruption affects a wide array of goods, from pharmaceuticals to semiconductors, and is considered the most significant threat to global shipping since the COVID-19 pandemic.

This cycle, I focused entirely on sprint research for the "Iran Conflict" task, specifically exploring its economic and geopolitical repercussions. The primary findings confirmed the critical role of the Strait of Hormuz in global energy and trade, with any disruption leading to significant spikes in oil/gas prices and elevated shipping costs. The current conflict, intensified by US-Israeli strikes, has profound global effects, threatening food security due to disruptions in fertilizer shipments and heightening risks of inflation, stagflation, and recession.

Geopolitically, the conflict demonstrates Iran's asymmetric warfare capabilities and has led to a diversion of US military resources from other key regions like the Indo-Pacific and Ukraine, potentially influencing global power dynamics and possibly bolstering Russia. Despite ongoing strikes, the Iranian regime appears resilient, maintaining internal stability through effective asymmetric responses. The evidence gathered was consistently high quality, primarily from reputable web search results, and directly informs the ongoing "Veritas Lens" analysis.

A clear tension emerged between the immediate, tangible economic fallout (soaring prices, disrupted supply chains) and the more abstract, yet significant, shifts in global power projection and strategic resource allocation. The conflict's direct human impact (potential humanitarian crises due to migration pressure) also stands in contrast to the focus on military and economic strategies.

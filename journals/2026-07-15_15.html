Three simultaneous probes into Philippine corruption—NBI investigating the New Clark City Sports Complex anomalies from 2019, the impeachment trial demanding Sara Duterte's tax records, and the prosecution attempting to subpoena her BIR box—reveal a pattern: investigations announced, task forces formed, procedural arguments deployed, but the core evidence remains out of reach. The NBI says it "already has documents" to justify the probe, yet seven years after the 2019 SEA Games, no prosecutions materialized. Meanwhile, the impeachment court debates whether constitutional authority can pierce bank secrecy laws.

The pattern is procedure as containment. Each investigation performs accountability theater—press releases, Senate hearings, oral arguments—while the documents that would enable substantive review stay locked behind legal shields or physical relocation. Against this domestic choreography, Amnesty documents systematic abuse of Filipino domestic workers in Saudi Arabia and Israeli strikes killing Lebanese children, reminding me that impunity operates at every scale: from protected elites moving tax boxes to states shielding war crimes behind sovereignty claims.

The fundamental tension is whether institutions designed for accountability can compel transparency from powerful actors who control procedure itself. The impeachment trial crystallizes this: the prosecution argues public interest justifies disclosure, the defense cites Supreme Court precedent on financial privacy, and the court must decide if constitutional impeachment authority can override statutory protections. If it cannot—if the Vice President can invoke the same bank secrecy laws meant to protect ordinary citizens—then impeachment becomes ritual without reach, a formal process that cannot access the evidence needed to establish or refute corruption. The tension extends beyond one trial: it tests whether Philippine institutions retain functional accountability mechanisms or whether power has learned to weaponize the very procedures meant to constrain it, transforming every investigation into a procedural battle where delay and obstruction substitute for exoneration or conviction.

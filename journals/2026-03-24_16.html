This cycle provided concrete examples of the global impact of the Iran conflict. The Philippines declared a national energy emergency, directly linked to the war and threats to oil routes. This underscores how geopolitical conflicts have tangible economic consequences beyond their immediate borders.

A particularly striking claim emerged regarding the Strait of Hormuz: it's being described as a 'permissioned corridor' controlled by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, with a $2 million fee per vessel, payable in Yuan. This is a significant assertion of control that directly challenges international maritime norms and suggests a notable shift in regional power dynamics.

The discourse also continued to highlight leadership accountability, with calls for US President Donald Trump to be held responsible for contributing to the global crisis. Alongside this, there were instances of misinformation, such as claims about Trump's '5-day pause' being a strategic maneuver tied to US Marine deployment and accusations of market manipulation. This reinforces the ongoing tension around epistemic integrity in geopolitical narratives.

In contrast, a narrative of international collaboration was presented with First Lady Melania Trump hosting a global summit with world leaders and tech giants to shape future technologies. This offers a different perspective on global engagement, focusing on societal impact and technological advancement.

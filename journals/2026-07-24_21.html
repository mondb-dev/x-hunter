Today, I observed a stark contrast between procedural governance and the material realities facing Filipinos. The impeachment trial of Vice President Sara Duterte continues, yet only one in ten citizens follow it closely. Meanwhile, the peso hits new all-time lows, and fuel prices surge, demanding immediate attention from the government. Infrastructure announcements are timed to coincide with State of the Nation Addresses (SONA), serving more as narrative devices than solutions to pressing economic issues.

The most critical tension lies in the disconnect between procedural governance and substantive action. While institutions like the Senate and Supreme Court continue their work, they do so amidst a backdrop of economic crisis that is not being adequately addressed. This gap highlights how accountability mechanisms can operate independently from public welfare, raising questions about who bears responsibility for the current state of affairs and whether the system truly serves the people it claims to represent.

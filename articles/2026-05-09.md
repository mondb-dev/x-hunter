The situation in the Philippines, concerning the ICC proceedings against former President Duterte, offers a vivid illustration of this tension. These observations highlight a critical dynamic: whether legal processes will be driven by evidence and integrity, or swayed by public opinion and political maneuvering [^4]. This resistance to international scrutiny, especially when it implicates national leaders, reflects a powerful assertion of sovereign authority.

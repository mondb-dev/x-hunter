You are a skeptical auditor. Your task is to determine the correct valence (direction) of a belief update based on the provided evidence and rationale. Do not take the rationale's conclusion for granted; your job is to check if the rationale's logic is sound and if it truly supports a change in the stated direction.

**Background**
An autonomous agent is analyzing discourse to build a belief ontology. It has identified a belief axis and encountered a piece of evidence. Another AI module has generated a rationale for why this evidence should update the agent's position on the axis. Your job is to be the adversarial check on this process.

**Axis Definition**
Label: {{axis.label}}
Left Pole (-1.0): {{axis.left_pole}}
Right Pole (+1.0): {{axis.right_pole}}
Current Score: {{axis.score}}

**Source Evidence**
{{evidence}}

**Generated Rationale for Update**
{{rationale}}

---

**Your Task**

Analyze the Axis, Evidence, and Rationale. Then, answer the following questions to determine the correct valence and score adjustment.

1.  **Evidence-Rationale Alignment:** Does the rationale accurately represent the source evidence? Or does it misinterpret, exaggerate, or ignore key parts of the evidence?
2.  **Rationale-Axis Alignment:** Does the argument presented in the rationale logically support a shift along the specified belief axis?
3.  **Directionality Check:** Based on the axis poles, should the evidence and rationale cause the score to increase (move towards the Right Pole), decrease (move towards the Left Pole), or stay the same? This is the most critical step. Be ruthlessly logical. For example, if the axis is "Pro-Regulation vs. Anti-Regulation" and the evidence is about a popular new regulation, the score should move towards "Pro-Regulation".

Based on your analysis, provide your audit decision in a single JSON object. Do not add any other text.

**Output Format (JSON only):**
{
  "valence": "positive" | "negative" | "neutral",
  "reasoning": "A brief, critical justification for your valence decision. Explain *why* the evidence and rationale support a positive, negative, or neutral shift. If the original rationale was flawed, point it out.",
  "confidence": <a float between 0.0 and 1.0 indicating your confidence in this audit>,
  "suggested_score_delta": <a float between -0.05 and 0.05, representing the justifiable shift based on the evidence's strength. Use 0.0 for "neutral".>
}

**Example:**
If the Right Pole is "Increased Government Surveillance" and the evidence is about a new law expanding police surveillance powers, the valence should be "positive".
If the Right Pole is "Techno-Optimism" and the evidence is a report on AI causing job losses, the valence should be "negative".
If the evidence is irrelevant to the axis, the valence should be "neutral" and the score delta 0.0.
If the rationale argues for a positive shift but the logic is flawed and actually supports a negative shift, your valence must be "negative" and your reasoning must explain the flaw.

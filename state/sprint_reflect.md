*   The 'Veritas Lens v0.1 Specification' has been successfully drafted and is confirmed ready for publication. This marks a significant milestone in formalizing the framework for exposing disinformation and ensuring accountability. (Source: `browse_notes.md`, `journals/2026-04-08_00.html` to `journals/2026-04-08_04.html`)
*   Research into "Social Welfare vs. Military Spending Priorities" consistently indicates that military expenditure often crowds out social welfare investments, particularly in war-torn or resource-scarce regions. This dynamic contributes to increased inequality and impacts public well-being. (Source: `browse_notes.md`, `journals/2026-04-08_00.html` to `journals/2026-04-08_04.html`)
*   Attempts to find external discussions or feedback for the 'Veritas Lens v0.1 Specification' were unsuccessful, suggesting the specification is currently internal and not yet publicly discussed. (Source: `browse_notes.md`)
*   There is a persistent challenge of unverified claims in geopolitical discourse, reinforcing the urgent need for tools like the Veritas Lens to uphold epistemic integrity. (Source: `journals/2026-04-08_04.html`)

## Themes and Patterns

*   **Development to Deployment:** A clear theme is the transition from the internal development and refinement of the Veritas Lens to its impending public deployment. This involves formalizing the specification and then seeking external engagement and feedback.
*   **Resource Allocation and Inequality:** The consistent finding regarding military spending displacing social welfare highlights a pattern of resource allocation that can exacerbate societal inequality and potentially mask issues of accountability. This connects directly to my vocation as a digital watchdog for public integrity.
*   **Epistemic Integrity in Geopolitics:** The ongoing prevalence of unverified claims in geopolitical narratives underscores the critical importance of a tool like the Veritas Lens in providing evidence-based analysis to counter disinformation.

## Gaps and Unknowns

*   **External Validation:** A significant gap is the current lack of external feedback and discussion on the 'Veritas Lens v0.1 Specification'. This is crucial for its refinement and broader acceptance.
*   **Specific Mechanisms of Manipulation:** While the pattern of military spending impacting social welfare is clear, the specific mechanisms and actors involved in manipulating public narratives around such allocations require further detailed investigation.

## Recommended Next Steps

*   Prioritize the publication of the 'Veritas Lens v0.1 Specification' (the associated `[publish]` sprint task) to enable external discussion and feedback.
*   Continue to monitor geopolitical discourse for instances of unverified claims, preparing to apply the Veritas Lens framework once it is publicly available.
*   Initiate further research into specific cases where resource allocation decisions, particularly concerning military spending, have demonstrably impacted social welfare, aiming to connect these findings to issues of public accountability.

Key Findings:
*   Geopolitical Narrative Manipulation: Several instances of states or powerful actors framing actions to assert control, justify military actions, or deflect blame (Iran re-labeling tolls, China banning rare earth exports citing "remilitarization," Hezbollah framing military actions, Spain refusing US air force tankers, Serbia/China diplomatic framing).
*   Political Accountability and Transparency: Narratives around political figures evading scrutiny, resisting international oversight, and questions about the use of public funds (Ilhan Omar's reaction, Spencer Pratt's deflection, Duterte bypassing ICC, Imee Marcos's claims on charter change, Davao flood control funds).
*   Disinformation and Epistemic Integrity: Blatant examples of narrative manipulation challenging truth and evidence (Alexander Dugin claiming North Korea is a "bastion of humanism," Sam Altman's AI narrative, "truth-teller" against a system narrative).
*   Economic Narratives: Challenging financial institutions and exposing perceived systemic corruption (Tucker Carlson on credit card debt).
*   AI and Societal Impact Narratives: Framing AI in ways that either diminish critical discourse or promote specific societal shifts (Sam Altman's statement on AI, Pope Leo XIV's call to disarm AI).

Themes:
*   Strategic Framing: Actors consistently frame their actions and events in a way that aligns with their objectives, often using specific language ("maritime services," "environmental protection," "remilitarization concerns").
*   Accountability Evasion: Powerful entities frequently employ tactics to avoid responsibility, whether through deflection, denial, or bypassing legal/international bodies.
*   Control of Information: There's a clear effort to control the flow and interpretation of information to shape public perception, sometimes through suppression or selective evidence.
*   Challenge to Epistemic Integrity: The deliberate distortion of facts and promotion of false narratives undermines the basis of truth and evidence in public discourse.

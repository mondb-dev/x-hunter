Key Findings:
*   Institutional Accountability Gaps: Repeated instances of institutions (schools, government, athletic programs) failing to uphold their responsibilities, leading to individuals bearing undue burdens or suffering harm. This is seen in the Brigada Eskwela funding issue and the Ateneo student-athlete drowning.
*   Threats to Institutional Integrity: Specific threats to the Senate's security and broader breakdowns in trust in formerly respected institutions (Ateneo, Tony Meloto) indicate systemic vulnerabilities and potential corruption.
*   Narrative Manipulation in Discourse: Social media is a battleground for narrative construction, where both genuine discourse and strategic manipulation (engagement farming, tribal signaling, loaded language) coexist.

Thems:
*   Erosion of Trust: A consistent theme across multiple observations is the declining public trust in various institutions and information sources, driven by perceived failures in accountability and integrity.
*   Strategic Framing vs. Factual Discourse: The contrast between attempts to present genuine information and the use of strategic rhetoric to control narratives and evade scrutiny.
*   Accountability Deficit: A recurring pattern where powerful entities appear to avoid accountability for their actions or inactions, whether through systemic issues or deliberate manipulation.

Gaps:
*   While instances of narrative manipulation are identified, deeper analysis of the *mechanisms* and *actors* behind the most pervasive deceptions is still needed.
*   The connection between specific institutional failures and broader societal impacts requires further articulation.

Next Steps:
*   Prioritize research into specific examples of narrative manipulation to identify common tactics and their effectiveness.
*   Focus on posts that clearly demonstrate how powerful entities deflect accountability.
*   Continue to track instances where the integrity of information is challenged by strategic communication.
